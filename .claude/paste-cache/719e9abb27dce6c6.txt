### 🔬 Why your "Random Starting Positions" matter
You hit on a key statistical point: even with "only" 161 files, your **effective diversity** is massive. 
* **The Math:** 161 stems $\times$ 180 seconds = 28,980 seconds of source audio. 
* **The Coverage:** You are extracting 10,000 samples $\times$ 5 seconds = 50,000 seconds of training data.
* **The Result:** You are essentially "recycling" each piece of audio about 1.7 times. However, because each visit applies a **unique, randomized 24-parameter EQ**, the model never sees the same "Delta" twice. This is the perfect defense against overfitting.

Once your generation hits 10,000, you'll move to the L4. Here is how that training session is likely to play out:

#### 1. The "Instrument Bias" Phase (Epochs 1–10)
