### 🔬 The "8-Band Overlap" Chaos
When you stack 8 random EQ bands, you aren't just adding Gain; you are creating **constructive and destructive interference**. 

* **The Masking Problem:** If Band A is at 1kHz (+6dB) and Band B is at 1.2kHz (-4dB), the resulting spectrogram shows a complex, smeared shelf. 
* **The Attribution Problem:** The model sees the "smeared shelf" in the difference channel. It can guess the *resultant* amplitude, but accurately attributing exactly how much gain came from the 1kHz bell vs. the 1.2kHz bell is mathematically "ill-posed" when they overlap.
* **The Noise Floor:** On real audio (drums/vocals), the energy isn't constant. If a frequency bin has zero energy (a gap in a vocal performance), no amount of EQ Gain will change the spectrogram. The model is essentially being penalized for "guessing" a gain that had no physical manifestation in that specific 3-second window.
