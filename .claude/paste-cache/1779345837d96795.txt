30 minutes is the "Golden Hour" of data engineering. It’s just enough time to grab a coffee, but not long enough to lose your momentum. 

At ~16-17 samples per second (which is what 30k in 30 minutes implies), your L4 is doing some serious heavy lifting. While the CPU handles the `pedalboard` DSP and Mel-transforms, the SSD is likely the one feeling the heat from writing those 30,000 `.npz` files.

### 📝 The "While You Wait" Checklist

Since we are jumping from a **97K** parameter model to a **55M** parameter beast, the "training feel" is going to change. Use these 30 minutes to double-check your `train_cnn.py`:

* **Memory Headroom:** With the new `flatten_dim` (53,248), your model’s memory footprint is significantly larger. If you hit a `CUDA Out of Memory` error at `batch_size=128`, don't panic—just drop it to **64**. The L4's 24GB is generous, but those large linear layers eat VRAM for breakfast.
* **The "First Epoch" Test:** As soon as the generator finishes, run a "fast_dev_run" or just watch the first 5 minutes. If the `train_loss` doesn't start dropping almost immediately, we might need to look at the learning rate.
* **Validation Frequency:** Set `val_check_interval` to maybe `0.5` or every `1.0` epoch. With 30k samples, you don't want to wait 20 minutes just to see if your validation loss is improving.
