## ✅ The "DO" List (Your Success Path)

* **DO Use `pedalboard.PeakFilter` for Cloud Generation:** Since Pro-Q 4 won't run on Linux, use the native `pedalboard` filters. They use the same JUCE-based math. 
* **DO Upload ONLY the "Dry" Audio:** Zip your raw `.wav` files and upload them to the Lightning Studio. Then, run your generator script *inside* the cloud to create the 10,000+ `.npz` files. This saves you 10+ hours of uploading.
* **DO Stick to the 2-Channel Input:** Keep the `[Wet, Dry]` stack. It is the only way the model can calculate the "Delta" required to predict Gain and Q-factor accurately.
* **DO Crank the `batch_size` to 128+:** The L4 has 24GB of VRAM. A larger batch size helps the model see a broader "variety" of audio in every step, which prevents the "hedging" we saw earlier.
* **DO Use Gain-Weighted Loss:** Keep that custom loss function! It tells the model: *"Focus on Frequency only when there is actually a Boost/Cut to look at."*
* **DO Monitor the "Val Loss" vs. "Train Loss":** If your training loss is $0.01$ but your validation loss is $0.10$, you are overfitting. This means you need to generate **more data** (50,000 samples instead of 10,000).

---

## ❌ The "DON'T" List (The Trap Avoidance)

* **DON'T Try to Force FabFilter on Linux:** You will waste a day trying to get `Wine` or `yabridge` to work on a headless server. Use the native Linux tools to keep your velocity high.
* **DON'T Upload Individual `.npz` Files:** Uploading thousands of tiny files is an I/O nightmare. Always **Zip locally -> Upload -> Unzip in Cloud.**
* **DON'T Forget Logarithmic Frequency Scaling:** If you switch back to linear scaling for your frequency labels, the model will fail to accurately predict anything in the Bass (20Hz–200Hz) or Mids.
* **DON'T Use `Sigmoid` on the Gain if you exceed ±12dB:** Since your final layer uses a Sigmoid, your targets *must* be normalized to $[0, 1]$. If you accidentally pass a raw $+12.0$ value to a Sigmoid-capped loss, the gradient will explode.
* **DON'T Ignore Inference Testing:** Every 10 epochs, run your `inference.py` script. The "Loss" is just a number—the "Predicted vs. True" table is the only thing that proves the model is actually "hearing" the EQ.
