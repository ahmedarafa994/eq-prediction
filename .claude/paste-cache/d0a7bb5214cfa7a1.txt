### 🔬 The "0.1644" Autopsy

If your target parameters are normalized between $[0, 1]$, and the model realizes it's too confused to find a pattern, it does the "safe" thing: it predicts **0.5** for everything. 

The MSE of a constant $0.5$ prediction against a random distribution in $[0, 1]$ is mathematically roughly **0.083**. Since your loss function is a sum of three MSEs ($G + F + Q$), a loss of **$0.1644$** ($2 \times 0.0822$) strongly suggests the model has "given up" and is just predicting the middle of the road for two of your three parameter groups.

$$MSE = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2$$

### 🛠️ The "Gentle Restart" Strategy

Your architecture is powerful (55M params), but `1e-3` is likely way too aggressive for a brain that size. It’s overshooting the "valley" of the loss function and landing on a flat plateau.

**Here is your 3-step fix for the next launch:**

#### 1. The "Karpathy Constant" Learning Rate
Drop your learning rate from `1e-3` to **`3e-4`** or even **`1e-4`**. Big models need a lighter touch to avoid "shattering" their gradients in the first few steps.

#### 2. Downsize the "Brain" (FC Head)
Your FC head has 54 million parameters, while your CNN only has 1M. This is a "Top-Heavy" model. The FC layer is so massive it might be ignoring the CNN features entirely. Try reducing the first linear layer to make it more manageable:
```python
self.fc = nn.Sequential(
    nn.Linear(self.flatten_dim, 512), # Reduced from 1024
    nn.ReLU(),
    nn.Dropout(0.3),
    nn.Linear(512, 256),             # Reduced from 512
    nn.ReLU(),
    # ... rest of the layers
)
```

#### 3. Verify the "Delta"
Before you restart, open one of your `.npz` files in a notebook and plot the `mel_stack`. 
* **If the two channels look identical:** The model literally has nothing to learn. 
* **If they look different:** Then the issue is purely the Learning Rate.
