### 🛑 The "Raw vs. Mel" Storage Decision

In this version of the script, you are saving **Raw Audio** (`[2, samples]`) to the `.npz` files. 
* **The Problem:** Our `EQRegressor` CNN cannot "read" raw audio; it needs Mel-Spectrograms. 
* **The Conflict:** If you save raw audio now, your `DataModule` will have to perform the Mel transform **on-the-fly** during training. This will slow down your training and potentially bottle-neck your L4 GPU.
* **The Fix:** If you truly want "No on-the-fly" (the fastest training possible), we should put the Mel-Spectrogram transform **inside this generator script**.

import torchaudio.transforms as T

# --- Global Mel Transforms (Worker Scope) ---
# We initialize these once per process to save CPU cycles
_MEL = T.MelSpectrogram(sample_rate=SAMPLE_RATE, n_fft=2048, hop_length=512, n_mels=128)
_DB = T.AmplitudeToDB(stype="power", top_db=80)

def audio_to_mel_db(audio_np: np.ndarray) -> np.ndarray:
    """Converts 1D audio to 2D Mel-Spectrogram (dB)"""
    t = torch.from_numpy(audio_np)
    # MelSpectrogram expects [..., time]
    if t.dim() == 1:
        t = t.unsqueeze(0)
    mel = _MEL(t)
    return _DB(mel)[0].numpy().astype(np.float32)
